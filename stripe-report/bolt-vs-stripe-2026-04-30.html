About Chargeback Protection

This section explains why the top-line comparison carries a caveat, and how to think about the three scenarios that follow.

What Bolt provides today

Bolt's "Fraud Protection" product carries what Bolt calls a Zero-Fraud Guarantee: when a transaction Bolt has approved later turns into a fraudulent chargeback, Bolt absorbs the loss instead of Trail-Gear. Bolt funds this guarantee through the risk fee on Trail-Gear's monthly statements (~$3,746 over the 90-day window, ~$15,000 annualized at current volume). The risk fee is therefore liability insurance, not a fraud-screening fee.

What's in (and not in) Stripe's current quote

Stripe's quote includes baseline Radar at $0.05/tx, which is fraud detection — machine-learning risk scoring that auto-blocks the highest-risk transactions. Baseline Radar is fraud screening, not liability transfer. If a fraudulent transaction passes Radar's screening and the cardholder later disputes it, Trail-Gear loses the sale, the product, and pays the $15 dispute fee — Stripe does not absorb the loss.

To replicate the liability-transfer behavior Bolt's risk fee buys, Trail-Gear would need Stripe's separate Chargeback Protection product, not on the current quote:

  • Pricing: ~0.4% per successful transaction (per Stripe's published documentation; Trail-Gear's pricing to be confirmed with Stripe).
  • Coverage: Stripe reimburses the disputed amount and waives the $15 dispute fee — for fraud disputes only. Non-fraud disputes (e.g., "product not received," "not as described") are not covered.
  • Annual cap: Reimbursements capped at $25,000 per merchant account per year (US).
  • Eligibility: Stripe's published criteria require a minimum of six months of processing on Stripe before a merchant becomes eligible — meaning Trail-Gear may not be able to enable Chargeback Protection at launch. Confirming this is one of the open questions.

Trail-Gear's chargeback rate is far below any threshold that would put the merchant account at risk with the card networks. At an annualized exposure of ~$6,300:

  • Bolt's risk fee at ~$15,000/year is charging Trail-Gear roughly 2.4× the actual chargeback exposure to insure against it. That is normal margin for a chargeback-protection product.
  • Stripe Chargeback Protection at 0.4% on this volume would run ~$32,000/year — also more than the underlying exposure, but again, this is the cost of insurance.
  • Self-insuring on Stripe (skipping Chargeback Protection and absorbing the ~$6,300/year directly) is mathematically the cheapest option at Trail-Gear's current chargeback rate. The tradeoff is operational: the Trail-Gear team handles disputes when they happen and accepts variance month-to-month.

This is a judgment call for Anna and Daniel, not just a math problem.

How to read this

  • The Stripe self-insure row is mathematically the cheapest option at Trail-Gear's current chargeback rate. The tradeoff is that Trail-Gear takes on the operational burden of handling disputes and absorbs variance month-to-month.
  • The Stripe + Chargeback Protection row uses Stripe's published 0.4% rate as a placeholder. At this rate, the savings essentially evaporate — the comparison becomes a wash. This is the row most sensitive to the actual Chargeback Protection pricing Stripe quotes; if Stripe offers Trail-Gear something below 0.4%, savings reappear.
  • The Strict apples-to-apples row is the most useful number for direct rate comparison: Bolt charges 2.72% for processing (excluding chargeback protection); Stripe charges 2.51% for processing (also excluding chargeback protection). On this basis, Stripe is 0.21 pp cheaper, worth ~$17k/year — and that's the savings before any decision about chargeback protection is layered on.

The original "$32k/year savings" headline corresponds to comparing Bolt-with-risk-fee against Stripe-without-Chargeback-Protection — a comparison that overstates Stripe's advantage by treating Bolt's protection as a fee while not treating Stripe's missing protection as a cost. The three scenarios above are the more defensible framing.
